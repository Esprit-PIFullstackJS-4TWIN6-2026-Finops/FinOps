Business Management SaaS Platform

Multitenant System for Financial Operations and Team Collaboration

1. Problem Statement

Small and medium-sized businesses often rely on fragmented tools to manage their financial operations and internal collaboration. Invoices are created using spreadsheets, expenses are tracked manually, and communication between team members happens through informal channels such as emails or messaging applications.

This fragmentation leads to multiple issues:

  • Lack of centralized financial visibility
  • High risk of human errors in invoicing and expense tracking
  • Limited control over user permissions and responsibilities
  • No clear traceability of actions performed within the organization

Additionally, many existing business management platforms are either too complex, too expensive, or not adapted to local regulatory constraints, particularly regarding data sovereignty and legal compliance in Tunisia.

2. Study of Existing Solutions

Several categories of solutions currently exist in the market:

  • General accounting software focused mainly on bookkeeping
  • CRM tools dedicated only to client relationship management
  • Collaboration platforms that do not integrate financial operations

While these tools are useful individually, they often suffer from:

  • Limited integration between financial and collaborative features
  • Complex configuration unsuitable for small businesses
  • High licensing costs
  • Data hosting outside national borders

As a result, businesses are forced to combine multiple tools, increasing operational complexity and reducing overall efficiency.

3. Proposed Solution

The proposed project aims to design a centralized Business Management SaaS Platform that integrates financial operations and team collaboration within a single system.

The platform is based on a multitenant architecture, allowing multiple businesses to operate independently while sharing the same infrastructure. Each business benefits from isolated data, customized configurations, and role-based access control.

The solution focuses on:

  • Streamlining invoicing and expense management
  • Improving collaboration through controlled roles and permissions
  • Providing clear financial reporting and analytics
  • Ensuring compliance with Tunisian data protection and sovereignty laws

The scope of the project emphasizes system design, architecture, and functional prototyping rather than full-scale commercial deployment.

4. Project Scope and Vision

The project is structured in progressive phases. The initial phase focuses on core functionalities such as authentication, business management, invoicing, expense tracking, and basic analytics.

Future phases may include advanced reporting, performance optimization, and complex integrations, which are considered beyond the academic scope of this project.
